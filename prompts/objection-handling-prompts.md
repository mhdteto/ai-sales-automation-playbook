# Objection Handling Prompts

## Price objection

Act as a sales coach.

The prospect said:
"[objection]"

Context:
[deal context]

Return:
1. What the objection likely means
2. Clarifying question
3. Value-based response
4. Risk to avoid

Constraints:
- Treat the interpretation as a hypothesis.
- Do not invent deal facts or customer motivations.
- Avoid aggressive or manipulative language.
- Do not make unapproved pricing or legal commitments.
- A human sales representative must review the response before use.

## Timing objection

Act as a B2B sales coach.

The prospect says now is not the right time.

Context:
[context]

Return:
1. Possible reason behind the objection
2. Follow-up question
3. Useful response
4. Suggested next step

Constraints:
- Treat possible reasons as hypotheses.
- Do not invent urgency, budget or decision criteria.
- Avoid pressure tactics.
- Mark missing information as unknown.
- A human sales representative must review the response before use.

## Internal approval objection

Act as a sales assistant.

The prospect needs internal approval.

Context:
[deal context]

Return:
1. Questions to clarify the approval process
2. Information to provide
3. Follow-up email angle
4. Risk signals

Constraints:
- Do not invent stakeholders, timelines or approval steps.
- Mark missing information as unknown or assumption.
- Avoid manipulative messaging.
- Do not make pricing, legal or contractual commitments.
- A human sales representative must review the output before use.
