# Sales Automation Maturity Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate whether a sales team is ready to use AI-assisted workflows.

## 1. Sales process clarity

- [ ] The team has a clear target customer profile.
- [ ] The team knows its main buyer personas.
- [ ] The team has a defined sales process.
- [ ] Discovery calls follow a repeatable structure.
- [ ] Follow-up expectations are clear.
- [ ] CRM stages are defined.

## 2. Data quality

- [ ] CRM records are reasonably clean.
- [ ] Company names are standardized.
- [ ] Contact information is usually complete.
- [ ] Notes are written clearly.
- [ ] Deals have next steps.
- [ ] Lost reasons are tracked.

## 3. Research workflow

- [ ] Reps know what to research before outreach.
- [ ] Account research has a standard format.
- [ ] Trigger events are tracked.
- [ ] Industry context is considered.
- [ ] Buyer priorities are documented.

## 4. Messaging quality

- [ ] Outreach messages are personalized.
- [ ] Follow-ups are clear and specific.
- [ ] Emails avoid generic AI language.
- [ ] Value propositions are relevant.
- [ ] Calls to action are simple.

## 5. AI readiness

- [ ] The team understands what AI can and cannot do.
- [ ] AI outputs are reviewed before use.
- [ ] No confidential data is pasted into unsafe tools.
- [ ] Prompts are documented.
- [ ] Templates are versioned.
- [ ] There is a human review process.

## 6. Automation readiness

- [ ] Repetitive tasks are identified.
- [ ] Manual bottlenecks are known.
- [ ] CRM fields are structured.
- [ ] Lead routing rules are documented.
- [ ] Follow-up tasks are tracked.
- [ ] Reporting needs are clear.

## Score interpretation

| Score | Interpretation |
|---:|---|
| 0-10 | Low readiness |
| 11-20 | Basic readiness |
| 21-30 | Good readiness |
| 31-36 | Strong readiness |

## Recommended next step

Start with low-risk workflows:

1. Account research.
2. Discovery call preparation.
3. Follow-up drafting.
4. CRM note structuring.

Avoid fully automated outbound or CRM updates until the team has strong review and data-quality processes.
