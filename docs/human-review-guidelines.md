# Human Review Guidelines

## Why human review matters

AI can help sales teams move faster, but sales communication still requires judgment.

Bad AI output can create risks:

- Inaccurate claims.
- Generic messages.
- Wrong assumptions.
- Poor personalization.
- Confidentiality issues.
- Brand damage.
- Compliance problems.

For this reason, AI-generated sales output should always be reviewed before use.

---

## Review checklist for sales emails

Before sending an AI-generated email, check:

- [ ] Is the company name correct?
- [ ] Is the contact name correct?
- [ ] Is the role or title correct?
- [ ] Is the pain point realistic?
- [ ] Is the message specific enough?
- [ ] Is the tone natural?
- [ ] Is the call to action clear?
- [ ] Is there any exaggerated claim?
- [ ] Is any confidential data included?
- [ ] Does the email sound like a human wrote it?

---

## Review checklist for account research

Before using AI-generated account research, check:

- [ ] Is the company summary accurate?
- [ ] Are sources reliable?
- [ ] Are assumptions clearly marked?
- [ ] Are trigger events real and current?
- [ ] Are suggested pain points plausible?
- [ ] Are competitors or tools correctly identified?
- [ ] Are recommended outreach angles relevant?

---

## Review checklist for CRM updates

Before saving AI-generated CRM updates, check:

- [ ] Is the summary factual?
- [ ] Are next steps correct?
- [ ] Are dates accurate?
- [ ] Are stakeholders correctly named?
- [ ] Are deal risks clearly stated?
- [ ] Are action items assigned correctly?
- [ ] Is sensitive information handled properly?

---

## Red flags

Do not use AI output directly if it contains:

- Fake statistics.
- Unsupported claims.
- Invented customer information.
- Overconfident assumptions.
- Incorrect company details.
- Confidential information.
- Aggressive or manipulative language.
- Pricing or legal commitments not approved by the business.

---

## Rule of thumb

AI can draft.

Humans decide.
